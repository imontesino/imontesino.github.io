  1. An Inverse Kinematics Problem Solver Based on Screw Theory for Manipulator Arms
    Bartek Łukawski, Ignacio Montesino Valle, Juan G. Victores, and 2 more authors
    In XLIII Jornadas de Automática, 2022
    Abs

    [Abstract] Several methodologies exist for solving the inverse kinematics of a manipulator arm. Basing on screw theory, it is possible to efficiently obtain complete and exact solutions. An open-source C++ implementation of an automated problem solver of this kind is introduced, and a comparative with selected known algorithms is established using the TEO humanoid robot platform by Universidad Carlos III de Madrid. The Orocos Kinematics and Dynamics Library is used for geometry and motion-related operations

  2. Impedance based Gym environment for the IIWA Collaborative robot towards human robot interaction
    Ignacio Montesino Valle, Bartek Łukawski, Juan G. Victores, and 2 more authors
    In XLIII Jornadas de Automática, 2022
    Abs

    As rehabilitation physiotherapy is on the rise, so too is the desire for robust and adaptable robotic solutions to standarise and automate common procedures. Yet modern control techniques have still to migrate to human-interaction centered environments such as the ones required in physiotherapy. This is in great part due to the lack of learning environments that can leverage modern force controlled robots. The aim of this paper is therefore to introduce a new reinforcement learning (RL) environment for learning and training of a force controlled robotic manipulator both in simulation and in the real world. This problem can be divided into three components, each one depending on the last one. First, a robust control driver is required that bridges between the robots native programming language (C++) and the Python programming language, where most RL algorithms are implemented. Second, a cartesian impedance controller that can be fitted to work both in simulation and in the real world, abstracting away most of the complexity of rigid body dynamics. And finally the construction of the RL environment based on the ubiquitous OpenAI Gym interface.

  1. Multi-ZMP Based Whole-Body Controller Approach in Humanoid Robots
    Juan Miguel García-Haro, Ignacio Montesino, J Hernandez-Vicen, and 2 more authors
    In , Dec 2019
    Abs

    This article presents a postural control approach of a humanoid waiter robot. In previous research, two methods were proposed to address this complexity. The first was an improvement for the control of body balance (locomotion), and the second was a method to apply the classical concepts of body balance to transport objects in a tray (manipulation). This approach is based on the concept of a multi-ZMP evaluation system to control the object and the body stabilities. Both methods were developed independently, avoiding disturbances between them. The integration into a whole-body postural controller is a challenge for the performance of both methods, due to the significant influence between them.

  2. Hacia La Bimanipulacion Inteligente de Cajas Con Robots Humanoides
    J Hernandez-Vicen, Juan Miguel García-Haro, Ignacio Montesino, and 2 more authors
    In , Dec 2019
    Abs

    Debido al continuo incremento del comercio electrónico a nivel mundial, cada año aumenta más el volumen de mercancias y paquetes que son entregados a particulares. Todo este movimiento lleva detrás un conjunto de operaciones logísticas que generan beneficios económicos y que permiten que estos paquetes lleguen a los clientes. Sin ir más lejos, en 2018 la logística en España supuso el 7,8% del PIB. Las operaciones logísticas de cada país son medidas por el Banco Mundial por medio del “Logistics Performance Index” (Índice de Desempeño Logístico) y España del 2010 al 2018 ha escalado del puesto 25 a la posición 17. Para poder continuar siendo un foco de atracción de empresas en este sector, es necesario optimizar estos procesos y desarrollar nuevas tecnologías que nos permitan seguir siendo foco de atracción para empresas importantes. En este marco se ha pensado el desarrollo de la aplicación de bimanipulación de cajas con el robot humanoide TEO de la UC3M, perteneciente al RoboticsLab.

  3. Modelo Simplificado Para El Transporte de Objetos Sin Agarre En Humanoides
    Ignacio Montesino, Juan Miguel García-Haro, J Hernandez-Vicen, and 2 more authors
    In , Dec 2019
    Abs

    Recientemente, la investigación en robótica de servicio ha atraído el interés de la comunidad. Ejemplos como HERB, PR2 o Care-O-bot4 están haciendo grandes avances. No obstante todas estas plataformas se basa en bases móviles con ruedas. En la UC3M se desarrollo el humanoide TEO con intención de ofrecer estos servicios a la vez que mantener la movilidad de un robot bípedo. Esta configuración genera no obstante una gran complejidad dinámica que dificulta el uso de métodos tradicionales como los parámetros D-H o la dinámica de Newton-Euler. Para ello proponemos un modelo basado en la teoría de screws y los algebras de Lie. Con el objetivo de obtener un modelo mas robusto e intuitivo que refleje el complejo proceso del equilibro humanoide.
